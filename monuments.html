Famous Monuments

Home Styles Monuments

Kailasa Temple

Carved from a single rock at Ellora, Maharashtra. A marvel of monolithic architecture.

Brihadeshwara Temple

Located in Tamil Nadu, this Chola-era temple showcases grand scale and precision stonework.

Qutub Minar

Delhi’s tallest minaret from the Delhi Sultanate period, reflecting early Indo-Islamic design.

Konark Sun Temple

Located in Odisha, this 13th-century temple is designed as a giant stone chariot dedicated to the Sun God Surya.

Sanchi Stupa

An ancient Buddhist monument in Madhya Pradesh, built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE.

Vittala Temple, Hampi

Renowned for its iconic stone chariot and musical pillars, located in Karnataka, built during the Vijayanagara Empire.

Ajanta Caves

Rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments in Maharashtra known for their exquisite paintings and sculptures.

Mahabalipuram Temples

Shore temples and rock-cut structures from the Pallava dynasty, located in Tamil Nadu.

Khajuraho Temples

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its intricate sculptures and carvings depicting various aspects of life and spirituality, built by the Chandela dynasty between 950–1050 AD.

Chennakeshava Temple

The Hoysala architectural style came to its pinnacle during the temple construction, which was distinguished by the 5-tier star-shaped platform, detailed carvings, and significant religious iconography.

Victoria Memorial

Victoria Memorial is a grand white marble building inspired by both British and Indian styles, serving as a memorial to Queen Victoria and a repository of artifacts from the British Raj era.

Kedareshwara Temple

The Kedareshwara Temple is a prime example of transitional Western Chalukya-Hoysala architecture, specifically a trikuta (three-shrined) temple. It showcases a unique blend of architectural styles. The temple's layout, with its staggered square design and projections, is a hallmark of Hoysala architecture.
