Dravidian Style

Found mainly in South India, characterized by towering gopurams (gateways), richly carved stone walls, and a pyramidal vimana above the sanctum. Example: Brihadeshwara Temple, Thanjavur.

Nagara Style

Predominant in North India, identified by curvilinear (beehive-shaped) towers called shikharas, and usually no elaborate boundary walls. Example: Kandariya Mahadeva Temple, Khajuraho.

Indo-Islamic Style

Fusion of Islamic and Indian traditions, featuring domes, minarets, arches, intricate jaali work, and large courtyards. Example: Qutub Minar, Delhi.

Vesara Style

A hybrid of Dravidian and Nagara architectural elements, common in the Deccan region. Example: Joda-kalasha temple, Karnataka

Kalinga Style

Unique to Odisha, this style includes a deul (tower) and jagamohana (porch), often decorated with elaborate sculptures of deities and dancers. Example: Sun Temple, Konark.

Hoysala Style

Flourished in Karnataka during the Hoysala dynasty; known for star-shaped platforms, soapstone carvings, and intricately detailed ceilings. Example: Chennakeshava Temple, Belur.

Indian Rock-Cut Architecture

Temples and monasteries carved directly into rock faces and cliffs, often dating back to ancient Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain traditions. Example: Kailasa Temple, Ellora.

Indo-Saracenic Architecture

A colonial-era architectural blend of Indian, Islamic, and European Gothic features including domes, spires, and ornate facades. Example: Victoria Memorial, Kolkata.
